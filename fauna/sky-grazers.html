Sky-Grazers

Core Concept: Massive, bio-electric drifters that serve as living weather vanes and industrial resources.

I. Biology & Physics

1. The Anatomy of Flight

  • Appearance: Immense, bulbous creatures with thick, wooly hides. They resemble a cross between a manatee and a bison, inflated with gas.
  • Propulsion: They drift on wind currents but use Internal Gas Sacs and Static Charge to regulate altitude.
  • Diet: They feed on high-altitude airborne vegetation and, crucially, Static-Charged Clouds. They literally eat electricity to maintain buoyancy.

2. The Mystery of the "Upward Fall"

  • The Phenomenon: Sky-Grazers never land. When they die, they do not fall.
  • The Physics: Decomposition causes their internal gas sacs to expand uncontrollably, overcoming gravity. The corpse becomes hyper-buoyant.
  • The Graveyard: They "Fall Upward," drifting into the upper stratosphere to freeze in the void or be pulled into the gravity well of the Broken Moon (Shatterface).
    • Myth: "The Moon calls them home."
    • Reality: They are space debris.

II. Behavior: The Migration Cycle

They act as Biological Barometers for the Chaos Zones.

1. The "Green Lane" Instinct

  • Sensitivity: Grazers can sense the "texture" of the Chaos Flow.
  • Laminar Flow (Safe): If the flow is smooth, the herd enters the Chaos Zone to feed on the high-energy static within.
  • Turbulent Flow (Danger): If the zone is "meandering" or unstable, the herd diverts.
  • Pilot’s Rule: "If the Herd turns, you turn." Smart captains (Sky-Barons) use the herds to find safe passages that maps miss.

2. Predators: The "Zone-Maws"

  • Grazers are not top of the food chain. Massive, void-adapted predators (Sky-Rays or Void-Eels) ambush them inside the Chaos Zones.
  • The Danger: Airships "drafting" behind a herd for safety often get caught in the crossfire of a feeding frenzy.

III. The Shadow Week Crisis: "The Static Famine"

Shadow Week (Eclipse) is a survival crisis for the species.

1. The Starvation

  • Cause: The eclipse blocks the Moon (Shatterface). The "Purple Haze" fades, and atmospheric static drops to near zero.
  • Effect: The Grazers lose their primary energy source. They become lethargic and cannot generate the charge needed to migrate.

2. Drift-Hibernation

  • Behavior: The herds stop moving. They cluster in massive, listless groups in "Stable Zones" (e.g., above the Great Lake).
  • The Hazard: They become Floating Minefields.
    • Airships flying blind in the dark often collide with these silent, sleeping giants.
    • A collision with a Grazer is like hitting a soft iceberg; it destroys engines and ruptures hulls.

IV. The Economy: The Cloud-Harriers

The Faction: Roughneck Avians living on the Northern Outer Cliffs.

1. The Method: "The Frame-Drop"

  • The Trap: Harriers use wind-tunnels to funnel herds along the cliff face.
  • The Capture: Massive, counter-weighted steel frames drop from the cliffs, clamping the beast against the padded rock wall.
  • The Harvest: Shearers repel down to strip the wool and siphon milk before releasing the animal back into the slipstream.

2. Product A: Voltaic Fleece

  • Properties: Naturally conductive but resistant to Aetheric burn.
  • Storm-Grade (High Value): Harvested before Shadow Week when static is high. It is the only material capable of insulating Aether-Engines. Without it, ships electrocute their crews.
  • Shadow-Grade (Low Value): Harvested during Shadow Week. The wool is flat and lifeless. Sold as cheap winter clothing.

3. Product B: Ozone-Milk

  • Raw ("The Baker's Lightning"):
    • Usage: A stimulant and baking agent.
    • "Thunder Buns": Pastries that rise explosively due to static leavening.
    • "Static Bloat": Eating too many causes gas expansion. It is polite to let out a "Static Burp" (spark-discharge) to relieve pressure.
  • Refined ("Aether-Clear"):

    • The Secret: Fermented by the Quill-Kin (Porcupines) using Ghost Flowers (from Dust-Husk Riders).

    • Effect: A potent Anti-Chaos medicine that treats "Corruption Sickness" and mental fog.

      The Mechanics of the "Grazer-Gate"

• Ren the Instinct: You established that they sometimes fly straight through chaos and other times avoid it.

• The Physics: This aligns with the "Laminar Flow" concept.

    ◦ Safe (The Green Lane): When the flow of chaos from the Moon to the Prison is high-pressure and smooth (Laminar), the Chaos Zone is stable. The laws of physics inside are weird, but consistent. The Grazers sense this stability and enter the zone to feed on the high-energy "static clouds" found within.

    ◦ Unsafe (The Red Lane): When the flow is turbulent or "Meandering" (like during the approach to Shadow Week), the chaos is erratic. Gravity might flip randomly. The Grazers sense this "Dissonance" and divert, avoiding it like the plague.

• The Pilots' Maxim: This creates a golden rule for airship captains (especially Sky-Barons and Ghostwind Raiders): "If the Herd goes in, the door is open. If the Herd turns, you turn."

2. The Lunar Migration Cycle (The 49-Day Loop)

Since Shatterface (the Moon) orbits every 49 days, the Grazers likely follow a strict "Tidal Migration."

• Waxing Phase (High Flow): As the moon brightens and the flow of chaos intensifies, the Grazers ride the high-altitude "Jet Streams" of magic. They move fast, using the chaos currents to glide effortlessly between continents.

• The Shadow-Stall (Shadow Week): During the eclipse/Shadow Week, the flow stops. The "Chain" goes slack.

    ◦ The Behavior: The Grazers stop migrating. They gather in massive, circling super-herds in the "Stable Zones" (like above the Heartland or the Great Lake).

    ◦ The Hazard: This creates a massive air-traffic hazard. The sky is clogged with millions of tons of floating flesh waiting for the "wind" to pick back up. Airship collisions with "stalled" herds become the #1 cause of crashes during Shadow Week.
